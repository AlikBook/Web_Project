N°1 Louvre Museum

See more

The Louvre Museum, located in the heart of Paris, is one of the world's largest and most visited art museums. Established in 1793, it occupies the historic Louvre Palace and boasts a collection of over 380,000 objects, with 35,000 on display. The museum's diverse collection spans from ancient civilizations to the 19th century, featuring masterpieces like the Mona Lisa, the Venus de Milo, and the Winged Victory of Samothrace. The iconic glass pyramid, designed by architect I. M. Pei and inaugurated in 1989, serves as the museum's main entrance and is a modern contrast to the classical architecture of the palace.

N°2 Orsay Museum

See more

Situated on the left bank of the Seine, the Orsay Museum is housed in a former Beaux-Arts railway station, the Gare d'Orsay, which was built for the 1900 Exposition Universelle. The museum opened in 1986 and is renowned for its extensive collection of French art from 1848 to 1914. It includes an impressive array of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist masterpieces by artists such as Monet, Degas, Van Gogh, and Renoir. The Orsay Museum provides visitors with a unique opportunity to experience the transformative periods of art history, bridging the gap between classical and modern art.

N°3 Rodin Museum

See more

The Rodin Museum, located in the elegant Hôtel Biron in Paris, is dedicated to the works of the renowned French sculptor Auguste Rodin. Opened in 1919, the museum showcases a vast collection of Rodin's sculptures, drawings, photographs, and other artworks. Among its most famous pieces are "The Thinker," "The Kiss," and "The Gates of Hell." The museum's beautiful garden features many of Rodin's sculptures set amidst lush greenery, providing a serene environment to appreciate his artistry. The Rodin Museum not only highlights Rodin's genius but also offers insights into his creative process and influences.
